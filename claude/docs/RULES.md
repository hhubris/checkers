## 🏁 Official Checkers Rules

### (American / English)

---

### 1. The Objective

The goal is simple: **Capture all of your opponent's pieces** or trap them so they have no legal moves left on the board.

---

### 2. Setting Up the Board

* **The Board:** An 8x8 grid of alternating light and dark squares.
* **Orientation:** Each player must have a **light-colored square** in their bottom-right corner.
* **The Pieces:** Each player starts with **12 pieces** (usually Red vs. Black).
* **Placement:** Pieces occupy only the **dark squares** in the three rows closest to each player.
* **The Lead:** The player with the **Black pieces** always makes the first move.

---

### 3. Gameplay & Movement

Players alternate turns, moving one piece at a time.

| Move Type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Regular Move** | A piece moves **one square diagonally forward** to an adjacent, empty dark square. |
| **Capturing (Jumping)** | Jump over an opponent's piece to an empty space immediately beyond it. The jumped piece is removed. |
| **Forced Jumps** | If a capture is possible, **you must take it.** You cannot choose a regular move instead. |
| **Multiple Jumps** | If a piece lands a jump and can immediately jump another piece, it **must** continue the sequence in the same turn. |

> **Note:** Pieces are restricted to dark squares only; they never touch the white squares.

---

### 4. The Path to Royalty: Kings

When a piece reaches the "King’s Row" (the furthest row from the player), it is crowned a **King**.

* **Mobility:** Unlike regular pieces, Kings can move **diagonally forward AND backward.**
* **Capturing:** Kings can jump and capture in any diagonal direction.

---

### 5. Winning and Drawing

* **Victory:** You win when your opponent has no pieces left or is completely blocked from moving.
* **Draw:** If neither player can realistically force a win (e.g., both players are down to one piece and just chasing each other), the game is a draw.
